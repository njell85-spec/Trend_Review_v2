### 1. Effect of goal-directed fluid resuscitation therapy based on peripheral perfusion index on acute skin failure in elderly critically ill patients: A randomized controlled trial.

- PMID: 42469971
- Journal: Medicine (2026-Jul-17)
- Summary: In elderly critically ill patients, adding a peripheral perfusion index (PPI ≥1.4) target to fluid resuscitation roughly halved 7-day acute skin failure without a mortality change.
- 요약: 고령 중증 환자에서 말초관류지수(PPI ≥1.4) 목표를 수액소생술에 추가하자 7일 급성 피부부전이 약 절반으로 줄었으나 사망률 변화는 없었다.
- Why it matters: Acute skin failure is common and morbid in elderly ICU patients, and this trial suggests a cheap, noninvasive bedside PPI target may reduce it while improving lactate clearance and limiting cumulative positive fluid balance—an appealing add-on to standard macrohemodynamic resuscitation goals.
- 중요성: 급성 피부부전은 고령 중환자에서 흔하고 이환율이 높은데, 이 연구는 저렴하고 비침습적인 침상 PPI 목표가 젖산 청소율을 개선하고 누적 양성 수액균형을 줄이면서 이를 감소시킬 수 있음을 시사하여 기존 대혈역학 소생 목표에 매력적인 보조 지표가 될 수 있다.

- Setting: Intensive care unit setting; author affiliations indicate Hai'an People's Hospital and affiliated centers in Nantong, Jiangsu Province, China (per PubMed metadata). Enrollment January–December 2025.
- Sample size: 216 elderly critically ill patients enrolled; 204 analyzed in the ITT population (103 intervention, 101 control). Reason for the 12-patient reduction is not available in the accessible sources.
- Eligibility: Elderly critically ill patients requiring early active fluid resuscitation (MeSH: Aged, Aged 80 and over, Critical Illness). Exact age cutoff, severity scores, and exclusion criteria are not available in the accessible sources.

- Comparator: Conventional resuscitation group managed with fluid therapy based on routine indicators (macrohemodynamic goals) without a PPI target. Specific protocol details not available in the accessible sources.
