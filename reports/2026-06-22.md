### 1. Cefazolin for Methicillin-Susceptible Bacteremia.

- PMID: 42308484
- Journal: The New England journal of medicine (2026-Jun-18)
- Summary: Cefazolin is noninferior to antistaphylococcal penicillins for 90-day mortality in patients with methicillin-susceptible Staphylococcus aureus bacteremia while significantly reducing acute kidney injury.
- 요약: 메티실린 감수성 황색포도상구균 균혈증 환자에서 세파졸린은 90일 사망률 측면에서 항포도상구균 페니실린에 비열등하였으며 급성 신손상 발생을 유의하게 줄였습니다.
- Why it matters: While antistaphylococcal penicillins like flucloxacillin are traditional first-line therapies for MSSA bacteremia, they carry a high risk of nephrotoxicity; this large-scale randomized trial provides high-quality evidence that cefazolin is equally effective but much safer for the kidneys.
- 중요성: 플루클록사실린과 같은 항포도상구균 페니실린은 전통적인 MSSA 균혈증의 1차 치료제였으나 신독성 위험이 높았습니다. 이번 대규모 무작위 임상시험은 세파졸린이 신장에 훨씬 안전하면서도 동일하게 효과적이라는 고품질의 증거를 제공합니다.

#### Outcomes
- Primary Outcome: 15.0% (97 of 645 patients) in the cefazolin group compared to 17.0% (109 of 642 patients) in the antistaphylococcal-penicillin group. (Adjusted odds ratio (aOR), 0.81; 95% credible interval (CrI), 0.59 to 1.12; probability of noninferiority, 99.2%; probability of superiority, 89.8%.)
  - ⓘ Cefazolin successfully met the prespecified noninferiority criterion (aOR <1.2, representing an absolute mortality difference of <2.5 percentage points) compared to flucloxacillin/cloxacillin.
  - 세파졸린은 플루클록사실린/클록사실린 대비 사전에 설정된 비열등성 기준(aOR <1.2, 절대 사망률 차이 약 <2.5%p에 해당)을 성공적으로 충족했습니다.
- Secondary Safety Outcome: 13.9% (92 of 660 patients) in the cefazolin group compared to 19.6% (127 of 648 patients) in the antistaphylococcal-penicillin group. (Adjusted odds ratio (aOR), 0.67; 95% credible interval (CrI), 0.50 to 0.89; probability of superiority, 99.7%.)
  - ⓘ Cefazolin is superior to flucloxacillin/cloxacillin, significantly reducing the risk of acute kidney injury within 14 days.
  - 세파졸린은 플루클록사실린/클록사실린에 비해 14일 이내 급성 신손상 위험을 유의하게 줄여주어 신장 안전성 측면에서 우월했습니다.
- Takeaway: Clinicians treating MSSA bacteremia should favor cefazolin over flucloxacillin or cloxacillin. Cefazolin provides equivalent efficacy in preventing mortality while significantly reducing the risk of acute kidney injury (13.9% vs 19.6%), offering an optimized balance of therapeutic efficacy and safety.
