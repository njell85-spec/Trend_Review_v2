### 1. Landmark Versus Ultrasound-Guided Identification of the Cricothyroid Membrane: A Randomized, Prospective Trial.

- PMID: 42418849
- Journal: The Journal of emergency medicine (2026-Aug)
- Summary: In stable ED patients undergoing neck CT, ultrasound-guided and landmark palpation methods showed no significant difference in accurately locating the cricothyroid membrane.
- 요약: 경부 CT를 받는 안정적인 응급실 환자에서 초음파 유도와 촉진 방법은 윤상갑상막 위치 확인 정확도에 유의한 차이가 없었다.
- Why it matters: Surgical cricothyrotomy is a rare, time-critical rescue procedure where correct cricothyroid membrane (CTM) identification is essential; whether point-of-care ultrasound improves localization over palpation directly informs difficult-airway preparation.
- 중요성: 외과적 윤상갑상막절개술은 드물지만 시간이 중요한 구조 시술로 정확한 윤상갑상막(CTM) 확인이 필수적이며, 초음파가 촉진보다 위치 확인을 개선하는지는 어려운 기도 준비에 직접적인 영향을 준다.

#### Study Details
- Design: Prospective randomized controlled trial comparing two CTM identification techniques with neck CT as the reference standard.
- Setting: Single university-based tertiary care center; per author affiliations the corresponding department is The George Washington University (Washington DC, USA).
- Sample size: 67 patients enrolled and analyzed: 31 randomized to ultrasound-guided and 36 to landmark palpation.
- Eligibility: Adult patients receiving a CT of the neck, identified through the electronic health record. Mean age 54.7 +/- 18.2 years; 56.7% female; median BMI 26.7 (IQR 18.3-35.1).
- Intervention: Ultrasound-guided (point-of-care ultrasound) visualization of the CTM by emergency physicians; after identification a 1.5 mm radiopaque metallic pellet sticker was placed over the site. Exact ultrasound technique/probe orientation not detailed in the accessible sources.
- Comparator: Traditional landmark palpation identification of the CTM by emergency physicians, followed by placement of the same 1.5 mm radiopaque pellet marker.
- Follow-up: No longitudinal follow-up; outcome measured on the index neck CT by a blinded neuroradiology fellow or attending measuring marker-to-CTM-midpoint distance.
- Source basis: Abstract plus public PubMed and Crossref metadata; no PMCID available for open full-text fetch.

#### Outcomes
- Primary: Overall 37/67 (55%) accurate; ultrasound 16/31 (51.6%) vs landmark 21/36 (58%), p = 0.63 (p = 0.63)
  - ⓘ Neither technique clearly outperformed the other, and accuracy was modest (about half) with both methods.
  - 어느 기법도 명확히 우월하지 않았고, 두 방법 모두 정확도가 약 절반으로 낮았다.
  - Stats rule: A p value >=0.05 (here 0.63) means the observed difference between groups is not statistically significant; this does not prove the methods are equivalent.
  - p값이 0.05 이상(여기서는 0.63)이면 두 군 간 차이가 통계적으로 유의하지 않음을 의미하며, 두 방법이 동등함을 증명하는 것은 아니다.
- Takeaway: Ultrasound did not outperform palpation for locating the CTM in this stable, mostly normal-BMI cohort; clinicians should not assume either method reliably localizes the CTM, and both may be imperfect when a surgical airway is needed.
- 임상 포인트: 이 안정적이고 대부분 정상 BMI인 코호트에서 초음파는 CTM 위치 확인에서 촉진보다 우월하지 않았다; 임상의는 어느 방법도 CTM을 확실히 찾는다고 가정해서는 안 되며, 외과적 기도가 필요할 때 두 방법 모두 불완전할 수 있다.
