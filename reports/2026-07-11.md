### 1. Low-dose corticosteroids in severe pulmonary infection: a meta-analysis of randomised controlled trials.

- PMID: 42425738
- Journal: BMJ open respiratory research (2026-Jul-09)
- Summary: Low-dose corticosteroids reduced short-term mortality in severe pulmonary infection, with the clearest benefit in community-acquired pneumonia and longer steroid courses.
- 요약: 저용량 코르티코스테로이드는 중증 폐감염에서 단기 사망률을 낮췄으며, 지역사회획득폐렴과 장기 스테로이드 요법에서 가장 뚜렷한 이득을 보였다.
- Why it matters: Corticosteroid use in severe pulmonary infection remains contentious across CAP, COVID-19, sepsis, and ARDS; this pooled analysis quantifies a modest short-term mortality benefit and shows it is driven mainly by CAP and by courses longer than 7 days, informing bedside decisions in the ED and ICU.
- 중요성: 중증 폐감염에서 코르티코스테로이드 사용은 CAP, COVID-19, 패혈증, ARDS 전반에 걸쳐 여전히 논란이 있다. 이 통합 분석은 완만한 단기 사망률 이득을 정량화하고 그 이득이 주로 CAP와 7일 초과 요법에서 비롯됨을 보여주어 응급실 및 중환자실 침상 결정에 도움을 준다.

#### Study Details
- Design: Systematic review and meta-analysis of randomised controlled trials, with subgroup analyses (by infection type and steroid-course duration) and GRADE certainty assessment; PROSPERO registered (CRD42024627881).
- Setting: Evidence synthesis of RCTs in critically ill/hospitalised adults; the review was conducted by UK-based investigators (University College London and affiliated hospitals per author affiliations). Individual trial countries are not specified in the accessible sources.
- Sample size: 12 RCTs including 4622 patients and 1203 events analysed. Per-arm denominators and per-trial sample sizes are not available in the accessible sources.
- Eligibility: RCTs comparing low-dose corticosteroids (≤400 mg hydrocortisone-equivalent daily) to placebo or standard care in adults with severe pulmonary infections, including COVID-19, community-acquired pneumonia (CAP), pneumocystis pneumonia, sepsis, septic shock, and ARDS.
- Intervention: Low-dose corticosteroids defined as ≤400 mg hydrocortisone-equivalent daily. Specific agents, exact doses, routes, and durations per included trial are not available in the accessible sources; course duration was dichotomised as ≤7 days versus >7 days for subgroup analysis.
- Comparator: Placebo or standard care, as defined within each included RCT. Specific comparator regimens per trial are not available in the accessible sources.
- Follow-up: Primary outcome was short-term mortality (≤90 days); secondary mortality endpoints included 28-day and 30-day mortality. Exact follow-up per trial is not available in the accessible sources.

#### Outcomes
- Primary: Pooled OR 0.83 (95% CI 0.70–0.98; p=0.029; I²=2.0%; NNT 37.84; GRADE low certainty) (OR 0.83, 95% CI 0.70–0.98, p=0.029, I²=2.0%, NNT 37.84)
  - ⓘ Corticosteroids were associated with ~17% lower relative odds of short-term death; about 38 patients need treatment to prevent one death, but GRADE certainty is low.
  - 코르티코스테로이드는 단기 사망 상대 오즈를 약 17% 낮췄으며, 약 38명 치료 시 1명의 사망을 예방하나 GRADE 확실성은 낮다.
  - Stats rule: OR below 1 means fewer deaths with treatment; the 95% CI (0.70–0.98) does not cross 1 and p<0.05, so the effect is statistically significant. NNT is the number needed to treat to prevent one event. I²=2.0% indicates minimal heterogeneity.

- Takeaway: Consider low-dose corticosteroids (≤400 mg hydrocortisone-equivalent/day) particularly in severe CAP, where a course longer than 7 days is associated with a short-term mortality benefit (NNT ~28), while acknowledging low GRADE certainty and inconclusive benefit for other pulmonary infections.
