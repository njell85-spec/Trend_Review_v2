### 1. Medical Record Abstraction for Quality Improvement in Sepsis Care Using Artificial Intelligence: A Cluster Randomized Trial.

- PMID: 42348212
- Journal: JAMA network open (2026-Jun-01)
- Summary: Near-real-time LLM-generated SEP-1 feedback to ED physicians improved sepsis bundle compliance from 70.1% to 82.9% without changing mortality or ICU admissions.
- 요약: ED 의사에게 제공된 거의 실시간 LLM 기반 SEP-1 피드백은 패혈증 번들 준수율을 70.1%에서 82.9%로 향상시켰으나 사망률이나 ICU 입원에는 변화가 없었다.
- Why it matters: SEP-1 quality reporting is labor-intensive and delayed, so timely AI-driven chart abstraction with physician-level feedback could close the gap between measurement and behavior change—but this trial shows a process improvement without a demonstrated patient outcome benefit.
- 중요성: SEP-1 품질 보고는 노동 집약적이고 지연되기 때문에, 의사 개인별 피드백을 동반한 적시의 AI 기반 차트 추출은 측정과 행동 변화 사이의 간극을 좁힐 수 있다. 다만 본 연구는 환자 결과 개선이 입증되지 않은 과정 지표 개선만을 보여준다.

#### Study Details
- Design: Single-blind, unstratified, cluster randomized trial in which the cluster unit was the attending physician; effect sizes estimated with a mixed-effects logistic regression model (intervention as fixed effect, random intercept for physician). The trial registry (NCT07581340) lists masking as none and design as randomized.
- Setting: 2 academic emergency departments within the University of California, San Diego (UCSD) Health system, United States; conducted December 13, 2024 to July 8, 2025 (ClinicalTrials.gov NCT07581340, status COMPLETED).
- Sample size: 66 attending physicians randomized (clusters), who treated 301 SEP-1-eligible patients analyzed: 121 in the control group and 180 in the intervention group. The registry-reported actual enrollment of 66 refers to physicians, not patients.
- Eligibility: Clusters: all 66 attending physicians practicing in UCSD EDs working >3 shifts/month before study start. Patients: adults (≥18 years) evaluated in the ED with clinical severe sepsis or septic shock meeting CMS SEP-1 inclusion criteria, with sepsis time zero during the ED visit. Excluded: sepsis onset before ED arrival or after admission, insufficient documentation, transfers with sepsis care already initiated, and cases whose attending was not assigned to a study arm.
- Intervention: Intervention physicians received near-real-time, individualized feedback on SEP-1 performance generated by a large language model (LLM) that performed automated chart abstraction at the time of ED discharge. Exact LLM model, prompt design, and feedback delivery format/frequency are not available in the accessible sources.
- Comparator: Control physicians received standard sepsis quality feedback processes without real-time augmentation; per the registry this feedback is much less frequent and typically delivered 3-4 months after the patient interaction.
- Follow-up: SEP-1 compliance assessed up to 1 month after the event; 30-day mortality was a secondary outcome (registry also lists in-hospital mortality up to 6 months after the event). Patient enrollment spanned December 13, 2024 to July 8, 2025.
- Source basis: Abstract (primary), PubMed metadata, ClinicalTrials.gov NCT07581340, and Crossref metadata. No PMCID was available for open full-text fetch, so detailed methods such as LLM specifics and baseline arm balance are limited.

#### Outcomes
- Primary: Control 70.1% vs intervention 82.9%; absolute improvement 13.0% (95% CI, 2.5%-23.4%); OR 2.10 (95% CI, 1.15-3.81); P = .02. (Absolute difference 13.0% (95% CI 2.5%-23.4%); OR 2.10 (95% CI 1.15-3.81); P = .02 from mixed-effects logistic regression.)
  - ⓘ Physicians receiving real-time LLM feedback passed the SEP-1 bundle significantly more often, with roughly double the odds of compliance.
  - 실시간 LLM 피드백을 받은 의사가 SEP-1 번들을 유의하게 더 자주 통과했으며 준수 오즈가 약 2배였다.
  - Stats rule: OR 1 = no difference, >1 = more compliance with the intervention; CI 1.15-3.81 excludes 1 and P=.02 (<0.05), so the difference is statistically significant. The 95% CI for the absolute difference (2.5%-23.4%) excludes 0, confirming a real absolute gain.
  - OR 1 = 차이 없음, >1 = 중재군에서 준수 증가; CI 1.15-3.81이 1을 제외하고 P=.02(<0.05)이므로 통계적으로 유의하다. 절대 차이의 95% CI(2.5%-23.4%)가 0을 제외하여 실제 절대 이득을 확인한다.
- Secondary: Intervention 3 of 180 (1.7%) vs control 16 of 121 (13.2%) noncompletion. (Proportions only: 1.7% vs 13.2%; described as a documentation-sensitive component. No separate CI/P reported in the abstract.)
  - ⓘ Most of the improvement appeared in the fluid-bolus component, which the authors note is sensitive to documentation, suggesting better charting as much as changed care.
  - 개선의 대부분은 수액 볼루스 구성요소에서 나타났으며, 저자들은 이것이 문서화에 민감하다고 언급해 실제 진료 변화만큼이나 문서화 개선이 기여했을 가능성을 시사한다.
  - Stats rule: These are raw proportions; a lower percentage means fewer failures. No confidence interval or p value was provided for this component, so statistical significance cannot be formally judged from the available data.
  - 이는 원시 비율로, 낮은 비율은 실패가 적음을 의미한다. 이 구성요소에는 신뢰구간이나 p값이 제공되지 않아 가용 데이터로 통계적 유의성을 공식적으로 판단할 수 없다.
- Secondary: 92% agreement. (Raw agreement 92%; no kappa or CI reported in the abstract.)
  - ⓘ The LLM matched expert SEP-1 adjudication in most cases, supporting feasibility of automated abstraction, though 8% disagreement remains clinically relevant.
  - LLM은 대부분의 경우 전문가의 SEP-1 판정과 일치하여 자동 추출의 실현 가능성을 뒷받침하나, 8%의 불일치는 임상적으로 여전히 의미가 있다.
  - Stats rule: Percent agreement is the share of cases where LLM and experts concur; higher is better, but raw agreement does not adjust for chance (no kappa reported).
  - 일치율은 LLM과 전문가가 동의한 사례의 비율로 높을수록 좋으나, 원시 일치율은 우연 일치를 보정하지 않는다(kappa 미보고).
- Secondary: No significant differences between groups in ICU admissions or 30-day mortality. (Reported as no significant difference; exact event counts, effect estimates, CIs, and p values not available in the accessible sources.)
  - ⓘ The process improvement did not translate into measurable patient-centered outcome benefit, though the trial was not powered for mortality.
  - 과정 지표 개선이 측정 가능한 환자 중심 결과 이득으로 이어지지 않았으나, 본 시험은 사망률에 대한 검정력이 없었다.
  - Stats rule: When a study reports no significant difference (P>=0.05), it cannot confirm equivalence—especially in a small trial that may be underpowered to detect mortality differences.

- Takeaway: AI-generated, near-real-time SEP-1 feedback can meaningfully boost measured bundle compliance and offers a feasible path to automate burdensome quality reporting, but clinicians should recognize that much of the gain may reflect improved documentation rather than changed bedside care, and patient outcomes were unchanged in this small single-system trial.
- 임상 포인트: AI가 생성하는 거의 실시간 SEP-1 피드백은 측정된 번들 준수율을 의미 있게 높이고 부담스러운 품질 보고 자동화의 실현 가능한 경로를 제시하지만, 임상의는 개선의 상당 부분이 실제 침상 진료 변화보다 문서화 개선을 반영할 수 있으며 이 소규모 단일 시스템 시험에서 환자 결과는 변하지 않았음을 인식해야 한다.
