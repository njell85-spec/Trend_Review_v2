### 1. Paramedic analgesia comparing ketamine and morphine in trauma. A synopsis of the PACKMaN double-blind RCT.

- PMID: 42474064
- Journal: Health technology assessment (Winchester, England) (2026-Jul)
- Summary: In prehospital severe trauma pain, paramedic-administered ketamine was not superior to morphine for pain relief but was a safe alternative.
- 요약: 병원 전 단계 중증 외상 통증에서 구급대원이 투여한 케타민은 모르핀보다 진통 효과가 우수하지 않았으나 안전한 대안이었다.
- Why it matters: Morphine is the standard prehospital strong analgesic but carries risks of hypotension, respiratory depression, and dependency; this large double-blind RCT provides high-quality head-to-head evidence on whether ketamine is a better or at least equivalent, safe option for paramedics.
- 중요성: 모르핀은 표준적인 병원 전 강력 진통제이지만 저혈압, 호흡 억제, 의존성 위험이 있다. 이 대규모 이중맹검 RCT는 케타민이 구급대원에게 더 낫거나 최소한 동등하고 안전한 대안인지에 대한 고품질의 직접 비교 근거를 제공한다.

#### Study Details
- Design: Double-blind, randomised controlled, superiority trial (PACKMaN); also indexed as an equivalence/comparative study. NIHR HTA-funded synopsis.
- Setting: UK prehospital/ambulance setting. Author affiliations indicate West Midlands Ambulance Service University NHS Foundation Trust and Yorkshire Ambulance Service NHS Trust, coordinated by the University of Warwick Clinical Trials Unit.
- Sample size: 449 participants randomised: 219 (49%) received ketamine and 230 (51%) received morphine. Separate ITT/per-protocol/safety denominators beyond these figures are not detailed in the accessible sources.
- Eligibility: Patients aged 16 years or over with an acute injury who articulated a pain score of 7 or greater on a 0-10 numeric rating scale. Excluded: pregnant patients, prisoners, those unable to articulate a pain score, and anyone lacking capacity.
- Intervention: Ketamine titrated to effect, maximum dose 30 mg, administered by paramedics. Exact route and titration increments are not detailed in the accessible sources (parenteral prehospital administration implied).
- Comparator: Morphine titrated to effect, maximum dose 20 mg, administered by paramedics under double-blind conditions.
- Follow-up: Pain outcomes assessed acutely (Sum of Pain Intensity Difference). Longer-term outcomes assessed at 3 and 6 months, which experienced loss to follow-up.
- Source basis: Abstract and public PubMed/Crossref metadata; no PMCID available for open full-text fetch.

#### Outcomes
- Primary: Ketamine 3.5 (SD 2.8) vs morphine 3.4 (SD 3.0); adjusted mean difference 0.1 (95% CI -0.4 to 0.6; p = 0.7) (Adjusted mean difference 0.1, 95% CI -0.4 to 0.6, p = 0.7)
  - ⓘ Overall pain reduction was essentially identical between the two drugs; the trial did not demonstrate ketamine superiority.
  - 두 약물 간 전체 통증 감소는 사실상 동일했으며, 시험은 케타민의 우월성을 입증하지 못했다.
  - Stats rule: For a mean difference, 0 means no difference between groups; a 95% CI that includes 0 and a p-value of 0.7 (well above 0.05) indicate no statistically significant difference.
  - 평균차에서 0은 두 군 간 차이가 없음을 의미한다. 0을 포함하는 95% CI와 0.05보다 훨씬 큰 p값 0.7은 통계적으로 유의한 차이가 없음을 나타낸다.
- Secondary: Odds ratio 1.58 (95% CI 1.08 to 2.31; p = 0.019) favouring ketamine (OR 1.58, 95% CI 1.08 to 2.31, p = 0.019)
  - ⓘ Patients on ketamine were more likely to report the highest category of improvement than those on morphine.
  - 케타민을 투여받은 환자가 모르핀보다 최고 등급의 호전을 보고할 가능성이 더 높았다.
  - Stats rule: For an odds ratio, 1 means no difference; above 1 means higher odds with ketamine. The 95% CI (1.08-2.31) excludes 1 and p<0.05, so this is statistically significant.
  - 오즈비에서 1은 차이 없음을 의미하고 1보다 크면 케타민에서 오즈가 더 높음을 뜻한다. 95% CI(1.08~2.31)가 1을 포함하지 않고 p<0.05이므로 통계적으로 유의하다.
- Secondary: Hazard ratio 1.42 (95% CI 1.09 to 1.84; p = 0.009) favouring faster response with ketamine (HR 1.42, 95% CI 1.09 to 1.84, p = 0.009)
  - ⓘ Ketamine achieved improvement more rapidly than morphine.
  - 케타민이 모르핀보다 더 빠르게 호전을 이끌어냈다.
  - Stats rule: For a hazard ratio on time-to-improvement, 1 means no difference; above 1 means the event (improvement) occurs faster with ketamine. The 95% CI excludes 1 and p<0.05, so significant.
  - 호전까지의 시간에 대한 위험비에서 1은 차이 없음을 의미하고, 1보다 크면 케타민에서 사건(호전)이 더 빨리 발생함을 뜻한다. 95% CI가 1을 포함하지 않고 p<0.05이므로 유의하다.
- Secondary: Hazard ratio 1.28 (95% CI 1.05 to 1.56; p = 0.013) indicating morphine lasted longer than ketamine (HR 1.28, 95% CI 1.05 to 1.56, p = 0.013)
  - ⓘ Morphine provided a longer-lasting analgesic effect than ketamine.
  - 모르핀이 케타민보다 더 오래 지속되는 진통 효과를 제공했다.
  - Stats rule: This hazard ratio describes the risk of pain returning; above 1 means the effect wore off sooner (favouring morphine for duration). The 95% CI excludes 1 and p<0.05, so significant.
  - 이 위험비는 통증이 다시 나타날 위험을 나타내며, 1보다 크면 효과가 더 빨리 사라짐(지속시간 면에서 모르핀에 유리)을 의미한다. 95% CI가 1을 포함하지 않고 p<0.05이므로 유의하다.
- Safety: No evidence of a significant difference in serious adverse events between drugs (No significant difference reported; exact counts not available in the accessible sources)
  - ⓘ Ketamine appeared as safe as morphine in this paramedic-administered setting.
  - 구급대원 투여 환경에서 케타민은 모르핀만큼 안전해 보였다.
  - Stats rule: When a comparison shows no significant difference, the data do not establish that one drug causes more serious harm than the other, though safety is not the same as proven equivalence.
  - 비교에서 유의한 차이가 없다는 것은 한 약물이 다른 약물보다 더 심각한 위해를 유발한다고 입증되지 않았음을 뜻하며, 안전성이 곧 동등성 입증과 같은 것은 아니다.
- Takeaway: Ketamine and morphine offer comparable overall prehospital trauma analgesia; ketamine can be used safely by paramedics and may act faster, while morphine may provide longer-lasting relief—drug choice can be individualized by patient factors and contraindications.
