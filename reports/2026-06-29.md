### 1. Rapid sequence versus early surgical stabilization of severe chest wall injuries: a propensity score-matched analysis from a multicenter trauma registry.

- PMID: 42351245
- Journal: Critical care (London, England) (2026-Jun-25)
- Summary: In a German trauma registry, Day-0 "rapid sequence" chest wall reconstruction was associated with nearly threefold higher in-hospital mortality than Day 1-3 surgery, most likely reflecting residual confounding by indication rather than harm from early surgery.
- 요약: 독일 외상 레지스트리에서 입원 당일(Day 0) "rapid sequence" 흉벽 재건술은 1-3일째 수술보다 원내 사망률이 약 3배 높았으나, 이는 조기 수술 자체의 해가 아니라 적응증에 의한 잔여 교란을 반영할 가능성이 크다.
- Why it matters: Timing of surgical stabilization of rib fractures (SSRF/CWR) is a hot topic in trauma and critical care; this is the first large multicenter analysis to test whether pushing fixation to the day of admission improves outcomes beyond the established <72-h window, and it cautions against a blanket "Day-0-for-all" strategy.
- 중요성: 늑골 골절의 수술적 고정(SSRF/CWR) 시기는 외상 및 중환자 진료에서 중요한 쟁점으로, 본 연구는 입원 당일까지 고정을 앞당기는 것이 기존의 72시간 이내 권고를 넘어 결과를 개선하는지 검증한 첫 대규모 다기관 분석이며 일률적인 "모두 Day 0" 전략에 경고를 던진다.

#### Study Details
- Design: Retrospective cohort study using prospectively collected registry data, with propensity score matching (PSM) to balance baseline characteristics; observational and non-randomized.
- Setting: TraumaRegister DGU (TR-DGU) of the German Trauma Society, 2015-2023 dataset (project ID TR-DGU 2024-018). Per full text, participating hospitals are predominantly in Germany (~90%) with additional sites in Austria, Belgium, Finland, Luxembourg, Slovenia, Switzerland, the Netherlands, and the United Arab Emirates; analysis restricted to primary admissions in Europe. Author affiliations include Goettingen University Medical Center, Helios Hospital Schwerin, University Hospital Erlangen, and IFOM Witten/Herdecke.
- Sample size: Initial cohort of 34,659 patients with severe chest wall injuries; 9.7% underwent CWR. After exclusions (including 857 operative cases, 25.5%, with missing surgery date and deaths within 48 h), 2,498 operatively treated patients with a known surgery date were analyzed. Of these, 1,168 (46.8%) had rapid sequence (Day 0) and 567 (22.7%) had early (Days 1-3) surgery (remainder were Delayed/Late, described descriptively). Propensity score matching yielded 500 matched pairs (n=1,000) for the primary comparison.
- Eligibility: Inclusion: primary admission to a participating European hospital; age >=16 years; ISS >=9; serious chest wall injury (serial rib fractures involving >=3 ribs, AIS 3, or flail chest, AIS 3-5); survival >=48 h post-admission; documented surgical CWR (rib fixation) with a valid calendar surgery date. Exclusions: secondary transfers, reduced/basic documentation only, missing CWR date, and deaths within first 48 h.
- Intervention: Rapid sequence CWR ("Rapid"): surgical chest wall reconstruction/rib fixation performed on Day 0 (day of admission), often within the first 12 h per the introduction. TR-DGU records surgery as calendar date only, not exact time, so Day 0 vs Day 1 boundary misclassification is possible. Specific implants, plating systems, and operative duration are not available in the accessible sources.
- Comparator: Early CWR ("Early"): chest wall reconstruction performed on Days 1-3 after admission, within the generally recommended <72-h window. Delayed (Days 4-6) and Late (Day >=7) groups were described descriptively but excluded from the primary matched analysis due to survivorship bias.
- Follow-up: Outcomes assessed during the index hospitalization (in-hospital mortality, sepsis, MOF, ventilation days, ICU and hospital length of stay). No post-discharge follow-up duration is described in the accessible sources.
- Source basis: Based on the article abstract, the open-access PMC full-text excerpt (PMC13308483), and public metadata from PubMed and Crossref (DOI 10.1186/s13054-026-06155-x).

#### Outcomes
- Primary: 10.6% (Rapid, Day 0) vs 3.6% (Early, Days 1-3); nearly threefold higher (p < .001)
  - ⓘ Day-0 patients died about three times as often, but the authors attribute this to higher baseline risk and residual confounding by indication/survivorship bias rather than harm from early surgery; matched Rapid patients still had higher ISS and predicted mortality (RISC III 16.2% vs 10.7%).
  - Day 0 환자의 사망이 약 3배 많았으나, 저자들은 이를 조기 수술의 해가 아니라 더 높은 기저 위험과 적응증/생존자 편향에 의한 잔여 교란으로 해석함; 매칭 후에도 Rapid군은 ISS와 예측 사망률(RISC III 16.2% 대 10.7%)이 더 높았음.
  - Stats rule: A p value <0.05 is conventionally considered statistically significant; here p<.001 indicates the mortality difference is very unlikely due to chance, though statistical significance does not establish causation given residual confounding.
  - p<0.05는 통상 통계적으로 유의하다고 봄; p<.001은 사망률 차이가 우연일 가능성이 매우 낮음을 의미하나, 잔여 교란이 있어 통계적 유의성이 인과관계를 입증하지는 않음.

- Takeaway: Do not interpret this study as evidence that early (Day-0) rib fixation kills patients; rather, patients selected for immediate surgery are intrinsically sicker. Continue to aim for chest wall reconstruction within 72 h when indicated, individualizing timing to injury severity and physiologic stability rather than mandating Day-0 surgery for all.
- 임상 포인트: 이 연구를 조기(Day 0) 늑골 고정이 환자를 사망시킨다는 근거로 해석해서는 안 되며, 즉시 수술 대상으로 선택된 환자가 본질적으로 더 중증이다. 적응증이 될 때 72시간 이내 흉벽 재건술을 목표로 하되, 모든 환자에게 Day 0 수술을 강제하기보다 손상 중증도와 생리적 안정성에 맞춰 시기를 개별화해야 한다.
