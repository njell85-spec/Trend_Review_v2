### 1. Early urinary decompression and mortality in septic obstructing ureteral stones: a nationwide comparative-effectiveness study.

- PMID: 42399868
- Journal: BMC urology (2026-Jul-03)
- Summary: In a US national inpatient cohort, urinary decompression—especially within 24 hours—was associated with lower in-hospital mortality in septic obstructing ureteral stones.
- 요약: 미국 전국 입원 코호트에서 요관결석에 의한 패혈증 환자에서 특히 24시간 이내의 요로감압술이 원내 사망률 감소와 연관되었다.
- Why it matters: Septic obstructing ureteral stones are a time-critical urologic emergency in which source control by decompression is the cornerstone of care, yet high-quality comparative evidence on whether and how quickly to intervene is scarce. This large, nationally representative observational study quantifies the mortality benefit of early decompression and directly supports urgent ED/ICU source-control decisions.
- 중요성: 요관결석에 의한 패혈증은 감압을 통한 원인 조절이 치료의 핵심인 시간 민감성 비뇨기 응급질환이지만, 언제·얼마나 빨리 개입해야 하는지에 대한 양질의 비교 근거가 부족하다. 이 대규모 전국 대표성 관찰연구는 조기 감압의 사망률 이점을 정량화하여 응급실/중환자실의 신속한 원인 조절 결정을 직접 뒷받침한다.

#### Study Details
- Design: Retrospective observational comparative-effectiveness cohort study using nationally representative administrative data, with prespecified causal-inference methods (propensity score matching, overlap weighting, instrumental variable analysis, and landmark/time-dependent survival models). Reporting followed STROBE and RECORD guidelines.
- Setting: U.S. HCUP National Inpatient Sample (NIS), 2016–2022, approximating a 20% stratified sample of U.S. community hospital discharges. Per PubMed affiliation metadata, the authors are based at institutions in Egypt (Fayoum, Al-Azhar, Banha universities) and Saudi Arabia (Najran University), analyzing the US dataset remotely.
- Sample size: 9,172 adult hospitalizations analyzed (conservative 3,458 [37.7%]; ureteral stent 4,982 [54.3%]; PCN 732 [8.0%]); 228 in-hospital deaths (2.5%). For the decompression-vs-conservative PSM, 3,262 matched pairs (57.1% of 5,714 treated). PCN-vs-stent overlap weighting had an effective weighted sample size of 1,836. Sensitivity timing strata: early ≤24 h n=3,821 and >24 h n=1,893 (alternate coding 4,795 vs 919).
- Eligibility: Adults (≥18 years) hospitalized with sepsis or septic shock and obstructing ureteral stones (calculi) identified from ICD diagnosis fields, with procedure fields scanned for urinary decompression (ureteral stent or PCN). MeSH indexing includes adults, middle-aged, aged; both sexes (57.9% female).
- Intervention: Urinary decompression = initial ureteral stent or percutaneous nephrostomy (PCN) identified from procedure fields. Timing derived as 24 × procedure calendar-day, so values are multiples of 24 h; ≤24 h corresponds to day 0 and >24 h to day ≥1. No specific stent size, PCN caliber, sedation, or antibiotic details are available in the accessible sources.
- Comparator: Primary comparator: conservative management (no decompression, coded NONE), n=3,458. Secondary head-to-head exploratory comparator: PCN versus ureteral stent among decompressed patients. Timing comparator: late (>24 h) versus early (≤24 h) decompression.
- Follow-up: In-hospital follow-up only (index hospitalization); outcomes are in-hospital mortality and inpatient event/resource measures. No post-discharge follow-up is available in NIS/accessible sources. Mean length of stay 7.5 ± 8.9 days overall.
- Source basis: Abstract, PMC open full-text excerpt (PMC13335030) including baseline Table 1 and results, PubMed metadata/affiliations, and Crossref bibliographic metadata.

#### Outcomes
- Primary: Standardized (g-computation) treated risk 1.6% vs untreated 4.0%; RD −2.4% (95% CI −3.2% to −1.7%), RR 0.39 (95% CI 0.30–0.50), NNT ≈41. PSM (3,262 pairs) showed −2.5 percentage-point absolute reduction (p<0.0001), mean post-match |SMD|≈0.019. (RD −2.4% (95% CI −3.2 to −1.7); RR 0.39 (0.30–0.50); NNT ≈41; PSM p<0.0001)
  - ⓘ Decompression was associated with roughly 60% lower relative and 2.4-point lower absolute in-hospital mortality, meaning about 1 death avoided per 41 patients decompressed—consistent across matching and standardization.
  - 감압은 상대적으로 약 60%, 절대적으로 2.4%p 낮은 원내 사망률과 연관되어 감압 41명당 약 1명의 사망을 피하는 것을 의미하며, 매칭과 표준화에서 일관되게 나타났다.
  - Stats rule: RR of 0.39 means fewer deaths with decompression (below 1 = benefit); the 95% CI (0.30–0.50) excludes 1, so the association is statistically significant. For the risk difference, 0 means no absolute difference; NNT 41 = treat 41 to prevent 1 death. p<0.05 is the usual significance threshold.
  - RR 0.39는 감압 시 사망이 적음을 의미(1 미만 = 이익); 95% CI(0.30–0.50)가 1을 포함하지 않아 통계적으로 유의하다. 위험차는 0이 절대 차이 없음; NNT 41은 41명 치료 시 1명의 사망을 예방. p<0.05가 통상적 유의성 기준이다.
- Timing: Late decompression had higher odds of mortality (OR 2.17, 95% CI 1.38–3.41), and higher AKI, mechanical ventilation, and dialysis. Prespecified sensitivity coding confirmed lower early mortality (45/3,821 [1.18%] vs 48/1,893 [2.54%]; and 66/4,795 [1.38%] vs 27/919 [2.94%]). (OR 2.17 (95% CI 1.38–3.41))
  - ⓘ Waiting beyond 24 hours was associated with roughly double the odds of dying and more organ-failure events, reinforcing that faster source control is better.
  - 24시간을 넘겨 지연할수록 사망 오즈가 약 2배 높고 장기부전 사건도 많아, 더 빠른 원인 조절이 유리함을 강화한다.
  - Stats rule: OR above 1 means more events with the exposure (here, late decompression); OR 2.17 = about twice the odds of death. The 95% CI (1.38–3.41) does not cross 1, so it is statistically significant.
  - OR 1 초과는 노출(여기서는 지연 감압) 시 사건이 더 많음을 의미; OR 2.17은 사망 오즈가 약 2배. 95% CI(1.38–3.41)가 1을 가로지르지 않아 통계적으로 유의하다.
- Secondary/Exploratory: Adjusted mortality higher with PCN than stent (5.5% vs 2.8%; OR 2.05, 95% CI 1.31–3.09). Unadjusted: PCN 5.46% (40/732) vs STENT 1.06% (53/4,982). PCN patients had markedly greater acuity (AKI 75.8%, ventilation 13.5%, higher Charlson, more transfers). IV results directionally consistent but likely reflect residual confounding by indication/feasibility. (OR 2.05 (95% CI 1.31–3.09); unadjusted PCN 5.46% vs STENT 1.06%)
  - ⓘ PCN was associated with higher mortality than stenting, but PCN patients were far sicker; this is hypothesis-generating confounding by indication, not evidence that PCN causes harm—PCN remains essential when retrograde stenting is not feasible.
  - PCN은 스텐트보다 높은 사망률과 연관되었으나 PCN 환자가 훨씬 중증이었으므로, 이는 PCN이 해를 끼친다는 근거가 아니라 적응증에 의한 교란(가설 생성적)이다—역행성 스텐트가 어려울 때 PCN은 여전히 필수적이다.
  - Stats rule: OR 2.05 (>1) suggests more deaths with PCN, CI (1.31–3.09) excludes 1; but observational OR reflects who received PCN (sicker patients), so confounding by indication limits causal reading.
  - OR 2.05(>1)은 PCN에서 사망이 더 많음을 시사하며 CI(1.31–3.09)가 1을 제외하지만, 관찰 OR은 PCN을 받은 환자(더 중증)를 반영하므로 적응증에 의한 교란이 인과 해석을 제한한다.
- Secondary: Overall AKI 52.0%, mechanical ventilation 7.4%, vasopressor 6.4%, dialysis 1.9%; mean LOS 7.5 ± 8.9 days. LOS longest with PCN (11.0 ± 10.4 d) and shortest with STENT (6.7 ± 8.1 d). Late decompression was associated with higher AKI, ventilation, and dialysis. (Descriptive proportions and means; group differences p<0.001 in Table 1)
  - ⓘ Secondary organ-failure and resource-use outcomes moved in the same direction as mortality, but these were exploratory without family-wise error control, so treat as supportive rather than confirmatory.
  - 이차 장기부전 및 자원 사용 결과는 사망률과 같은 방향이었으나 다중검정 보정 없는 탐색적 분석이므로 확증이 아닌 보조적 근거로 해석해야 한다.
  - Stats rule: These are descriptive comparisons; p<0.001 indicates the groups differ statistically, but because outcomes were exploratory without family-wise error control, statistical significance here does not confirm causal effect.
  - 이들은 기술적 비교이며 p<0.001은 그룹 간 통계적 차이를 나타내지만, 이차/탐색적이고 다중검정 보정이 없어 여기서의 통계적 유의성이 인과 효과를 확증하지 않는다.
- Takeaway: For septic obstructing ureteral stones, pursue urgent source control—decompress as early as feasible, ideally within 24 hours—since delay is associated with substantially higher mortality and organ failure. Choose stent vs PCN based on clinical feasibility, not on this study's exploratory modality signal.
- 임상 포인트: 요관결석에 의한 패혈증에서는 신속한 원인 조절을 추구하라—가능한 한 조기에, 이상적으로 24시간 이내에 감압하라. 지연은 훨씬 높은 사망률 및 장기부전과 연관되기 때문이다. 스텐트 대 PCN 선택은 이 연구의 탐색적 방식 신호가 아니라 임상적 실행가능성에 근거해야 한다.
