### 1. Mortality After Piperacillin-Tazobactam-Resistant vs -Susceptible Bloodstream Infection.

- PMID: 42485042
- Journal: JAMA network open (2026-Jul-01)
- Summary: In a nationwide Danish cohort, 30-day mortality did not differ significantly between piperacillin-tazobactam-resistant and -susceptible E coli or K pneumoniae bloodstream infections after adjusting for baseline risk.
- 요약: 덴마크 전국 코호트에서 기저 위험 보정 후 piperacillin-tazobactam 내성과 감수성 E coli 또는 K pneumoniae 혈류감염 간 30일 사망률에 유의한 차이가 없었다.
- Why it matters: TZP is widely used empirically for gram-negative bloodstream infections, and TZP resistance is rising across Europe; knowing whether resistance itself independently worsens mortality helps clinicians weigh empiric coverage decisions and antimicrobial stewardship.
- 중요성: TZP는 그람음성 혈류감염에 경험적으로 널리 사용되며 유럽 전역에서 TZP 내성이 증가하고 있다. 내성 자체가 독립적으로 사망률을 악화시키는지 아는 것은 임상의가 경험적 항균 커버리지 결정과 항균제 관리를 판단하는 데 도움이 된다.

#### Study Details
- Design: Nationwide observational cohort study using Danish national health registers with near real-time data collection; univariable and multivariable Cox models estimating 30-day mortality hazard ratios from index blood culture until death, emigration, or end of observation.
- Setting: Denmark, national health registers spanning November 15, 2018 through November 21, 2024. Author affiliations include Statens Serum Institut (Copenhagen) and clinical microbiology departments at Herlev/Gentofte, Odense, and Aalborg University Hospitals (public metadata).
- Sample size: 34,379 patients analyzed overall: 28,649 (81.9%) with E coli BSI (of whom 1,689 [5.9%] TZP-resistant) and 6,314 (18.1%) with K pneumoniae BSI (of whom 597 [9.5%] TZP-resistant). No loss to follow-up; ~1% right-censored at end of observation (E coli 343 [1.2%]; K pneumoniae 88 [1.4%]).
- Eligibility: Patients aged 18 years or older with monomicrobial E coli or K pneumoniae BSI, restricted to the first positive (index) blood culture with a TZP-susceptibility test result during the follow-up period.
- Intervention: This is an exposure-based observational study, not a treatment trial. The exposure was a TZP susceptibility test classified as TZP resistant. Actual antibiotic therapy administered, dosing, route, and duration are not available in the accessible sources.
- Comparator: Comparison group was BSI with a TZP-susceptible susceptibility test result, within the same E coli or K pneumoniae species strata.
- Follow-up: 30-day mortality from index blood culture date; follow-up until death, emigration, or end of observation period (November 21, 2024). No loss to follow-up reported.
- Source basis: Based on the JAMA Network Open abstract plus public PubMed and Crossref metadata; no PMCID available for open full-text, so granular methods (e.g., resistance breakpoints, actual treatment received, baseline covariate balance) beyond the abstract are not accessible.

#### Outcomes

- Takeaway: TZP resistance in E coli or K pneumoniae bloodstream infection does not appear to independently increase 30-day mortality; observed differences are largely driven by sicker baseline patients—reinforcing the importance of prompt appropriate therapy and stewardship rather than assuming resistance alone drives worse outcomes.
- 임상 포인트: E coli 또는 K pneumoniae 혈류감염의 TZP 내성은 30일 사망률을 독립적으로 증가시키지 않는 것으로 보이며, 관찰된 차이는 대체로 더 중한 기저 환자에 기인한다. 이는 내성 자체가 나쁜 결과를 유발한다고 가정하기보다 신속한 적절 치료와 항균제 관리의 중요성을 강조한다.
