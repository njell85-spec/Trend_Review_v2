### 1. Procalcitonin to Guide 7 vs 14 Days of Antibiotics in Bloodstream Infections: A Secondary Analysis of the BALANCE Trial.

- PMID: 42371622
- Journal: JAMA network open (2026-Jun-01)
- Summary: A day-7 procalcitonin ≥250 pg/mL flagged higher 90-day mortality in bacteremia, but prolonging antibiotics to 14 days did not improve survival in those high-PCT patients.
- 요약: 7일째 프로칼시토닌 ≥250 pg/mL은 균혈증에서 90일 사망률 증가와 연관됐지만, 이 고PCT 환자에서 항생제를 14일로 연장해도 생존을 개선하지 못했다.
- Why it matters: Clinicians often feel uneasy stopping antibiotics at 7 days when a patient still looks inflamed. This secondary analysis tests whether a residually elevated day-7 PCT identifies bacteremia patients who would benefit from a longer course, directly informing antibiotic stewardship and the durability of the BALANCE 7-day strategy.
- 중요성: 임상의는 7일째에도 환자가 여전히 염증 소견을 보일 때 항생제 중단을 망설인다. 이 이차 분석은 7일째 잔존 상승된 PCT가 더 긴 치료에서 이득을 볼 균혈증 환자를 식별하는지 검증하여, 항생제 관리와 BALANCE 7일 전략의 견고성에 직접적인 정보를 제공한다.

#### Study Details
- Design: Planned secondary analysis (cohort study) of the multicenter, randomized, noninferiority BALANCE trial; day-7 serum procalcitonin measured retrospectively by an antibody-based assay and blinded to treating clinicians.
- Setting: BALANCE was conducted from 2014 to 2023; per author affiliations this was a Canadian multicenter effort (Toronto, Montreal, Sherbrooke, Calgary). The exact number of contributing sites for this PCT substudy is not available in the accessible sources.
- Sample size: 125 patients with available day-7 serum were included and analyzed: 65 (52%) with low PCT (<250 pg/mL) and 60 (48%) with high PCT (≥250 pg/mL). This is the analyzed subset of the larger BALANCE trial, not the full BALANCE randomized cohort.
- Eligibility: Patients enrolled in the BALANCE trial with bacteremia who had a serum sample collected on day 7 after antibiotic initiation. Median age 63 years (IQR 58-79); 80 (64.0%) male. Specific microbiology and exclusion criteria for the substudy are not detailed in the accessible sources.
- Intervention: Randomization to 7 days of antibiotics. Antibiotic selection, dosing, and route were at the discretion of the treating team; specific agents are not available in the accessible sources.
- Comparator: Randomization to 14 days of antibiotics, also with agent/dose/route at the treating team's discretion.
- Follow-up: Patients were followed for 90 days after study enrollment for the primary mortality outcome; secondary outcomes assessed within 28 days of the index positive blood culture. Substudy data analyzed January to June 2025.
- Source basis: Abstract and PubMed/Crossref metadata; no PMCID/open full text was available, so site counts and microbiology specifics could not be confirmed.

#### Outcomes
- Primary: 21.6% (13/60) in high PCT vs 6.2% (4/65) in low PCT; ARD 15.5% (95% CI, 3.6%-27.5%) (ARD 15.5%, 95% CI 3.6%-27.5%)
  - ⓘ Patients with a residually high day-7 PCT had roughly 3-fold higher 90-day mortality; the confidence interval excludes 0, so the prognostic association is statistically robust. This reflects sicker, older patients rather than proven causation.
  - 7일째 잔존 고PCT 환자는 90일 사망률이 약 3배 높았고, 신뢰구간이 0을 포함하지 않아 예후 연관성이 통계적으로 견고하다. 이는 인과관계 입증이 아니라 더 중증·고령 환자를 반영한다.
  - Stats rule: Absolute risk difference (ARD): 0 means no difference between groups; a 95% CI that does not cross 0 indicates a statistically significant difference at the conventional level.
  - 절대위험차(ARD): 0은 군 간 차이 없음을 의미하며, 0을 포함하지 않는 95% CI는 통상적 수준에서 통계적으로 유의함을 나타낸다.
- Key subgroup: ARD -19.9% (95% CI, -1.7 to 41.7) (ARD -19.9%, 95% CI -1.7 to 41.7)
  - ⓘ Within the high-PCT group, longer therapy was not associated with lower mortality; the wide confidence interval crosses 0, so no benefit from prolonging antibiotics to 14 days was demonstrated. The small subgroup makes this estimate imprecise.

  - Stats rule: For an ARD, 0 means no absolute difference; when the 95% CI spans 0, a benefit or harm is not statistically established, especially in small subgroups.

- Takeaway: Do not use a high day-7 procalcitonin to justify extending antibiotics beyond 7 days in bacteremia; it signals a sicker patient prognostically but did not identify a group that benefited from 14 days. Continue to apply the BALANCE 7-day strategy and address the underlying severity/source rather than reflexively prolonging therapy.
