### 1. High-Flow Nasal Cannula in Hypercapnic Respiratory Failure: An Updated Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis.

- PMID: 42387305
- Journal: The clinical respiratory journal (2026-Jul)
- Summary: In adults with acute hypercapnic respiratory failure, HFNC and BiPAP yield comparable intubation and mortality, with BiPAP giving minor physiologic gains and HFNC better comfort and fewer complications.
- 요약: 급성 고탄산혈증 호흡부전 성인에서 HFNC와 BiPAP는 삽관율·사망률이 유사하며, BiPAP는 경미한 생리학적 이점을, HFNC는 더 나은 편안함과 적은 합병증을 제공한다.
- Why it matters: Clinicians frequently choose between HFNC and BiPAP for hypercapnic respiratory failure (especially AECOPD), and this updated pooled analysis suggests HFNC may be a reasonable, better-tolerated alternative when BiPAP is poorly tolerated, without a signal of worse intubation or death.
- 중요성: 임상의는 고탄산혈증 호흡부전(특히 AECOPD)에서 HFNC와 BiPAP 사이에서 자주 선택하는데, 이 업데이트된 통합 분석은 BiPAP 내약성이 나쁠 때 HFNC가 삽관·사망 악화 신호 없이 합리적이고 내약성이 더 좋은 대안이 될 수 있음을 시사한다.

#### Study Details
- Design: Updated systematic review and meta-analysis of parallel-group RCTs; PROSPERO-registered (CRD420251048698), PRISMA-reported. Dichotomous outcomes pooled as risk differences (RD), continuous as mean differences (MD) or standardized mean differences (SMD); fixed- or random-effects per heterogeneity (I2>50% substantial), with meta-regression, sensitivity, leave-one-out, and trim-and-fill analyses.
- Setting: Databases searched (PubMed, Web of Science, Cochrane CENTRAL, CNKI, Wanfang) through June 2025. Included trials ran in emergency departments, ICUs, or respiratory ICUs (RICUs). Three were multicenter and eight single-center; six of eleven studies (54.5%) were conducted in China (four in Chinese, two in English), the remainder in other countries. Authors are from Soochow University-affiliated hospitals, Suzhou, China.
- Sample size: 11 parallel-group RCTs with 1069 total participants included in quantitative synthesis. Outcome-specific denominators: intubation 7 RCTs (n=721); mortality 8 RCTs (n=761); PaCO2 and pH 8 RCTs (n=902); oxygenation 6 RCTs (n=660); respiratory rate 5 RCTs (n=497); ICU stay 4 RCTs; comfort/complications 9 RCTs. Individual arm denominators not detailed in accessible sources.
- Eligibility: Adults aged >=16 years with acute hypercapnic respiratory failure (PaCO2 > 45 mmHg) randomized to HFNC vs BiPAP in parallel-group RCTs. Most cohorts were AECOPD; one study did not specify etiology. Excluded: overlapping/duplicate data, studies missing predefined outcomes, non-RCTs, and patients weaning from invasive mechanical ventilation.
- Intervention: High-flow nasal cannula (HFNC) delivering heated, humidified oxygen; flow rates across trials ranged from 25 to 60 L/min, titrated to patient comfort. One included trial (Haciosman et al.) used a 50-L/min subgroup and was a notable source of heterogeneity for PaCO2/pH.
- Comparator: Bi-level positive airway pressure (BiPAP) delivering noninvasive positive-pressure ventilation via nasal, oronasal, or full-face interfaces. Specific IPAP/EPAP pressure settings are not detailed in the accessible sources.
- Follow-up: Blood-gas and respiratory-rate outcomes were assessed at approximately 48 hours after intervention initiation. Overall follow-up duration varied by included trial and is not uniformly specified in the accessible sources.
- Source basis: Derived from the PMC open full text (PMC13323174), PubMed metadata, and Crossref metadata for the abstract and pooled estimates.

  - ⓘ Death rates were comparable between the two therapies, with the interval spanning zero.
